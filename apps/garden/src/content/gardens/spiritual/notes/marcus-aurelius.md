Marcus Aurelius (121-180 AD) was Roman Emperor from 161 to 180 AD and a practitioner of [[Stoicism|stoic philosophy]]. He is best known for his personal writings, commonly known as *Meditations*, which he composed during military campaigns.

## Key Ideas

His writings emphasize:

- The impermanence of all things ([[Memento Mori]])
- The importance of rational self-discipline
- Service to the common good as a leader's duty

## Relevance Today

Marcus Aurelius is often cited in modern [[Stoicism|stoic]] circles and leadership literature as an example of philosophical practice applied to governance.
