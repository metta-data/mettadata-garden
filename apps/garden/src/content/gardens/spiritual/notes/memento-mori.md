Memento Mori is a Latin phrase meaning "remember that you will die." In [[Stoicism|stoic philosophy]], it serves as a meditative practice to maintain perspective and urgency in living a virtuous life.

## Practice

The Stoics, including [[Marcus Aurelius]], used memento mori as a daily reflection:
- To avoid procrastination on meaningful work
- To release attachment to material possessions
- To deepen gratitude for the present moment

- [[Amor Fati]] — loving one's fate, including mortality
